What Every Professional Invoice Needs

üìã The Complete Invoice Checklist

  • Your business name and logo
  • Your contact information (phone, email, address)
  • License number (where applicable)
  • Invoice number (for tracking)
  • Invoice date
  • Client name and address
  • Itemized list of services
  • Subtotal, tax, and total

Invoice Design Tips That Work

1. Keep It Clean and Scannable

Clients should understand your invoice in 5 seconds. Use clear headings, white space, and make the total impossible to miss.

2. Use Your Brand Colors

Even a simple color accent makes your invoice look 10x more professional than plain black and white.

3. Make the Total Stand Out

Use a larger font, bold text, or colored background for the amount due.

üí° Pro Tip: Photo Documentation

For larger jobs, include photos of completed work. This reinforces value and reduces disputes. Many contractors using Toolbelt attach before/after photos directly to their PDF invoices.

Trade-Specific Considerations

Plumbers

  • Include permit numbers when applicable
  • List parts with model numbers for warranty

Electricians

  • License number is critical (legally required)
  • Document code compliance

General Contractors

  • Break down by project phase
  • Attach change orders as supporting documents

The Bottom Line

Your invoice is a marketing document. Every one you send reinforces your professionalism. Invest 30 minutes setting up a professional template ‚Äî you'll benefit for years.
